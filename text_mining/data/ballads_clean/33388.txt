/ 2 [ THE Coy CO ] OK-MAID , Who was Courted importunately by Irish , Welch , Spanish , French and Dutch , but at last was conquered by a poor English Taylor . To the Tune of , There was a brisk Lass , etc This may be Printed , R.P. [ J ] Oan scrub'd up her rooms , made all things clean , The tables , the chairs , and the edge of the skreen , [ S ] he scour'd each pisspot and pewter-dish [ Ma ] de e'ry thing clean as heart could wish ; [ That ] e pewter and brass was so very clear , [ That ] at wanting a glass , she oft drew near , [ To ] deck up her head and curl her hair , [ Not ] one among twenty with her could compare . [ She ] made her plumb pottage and sweet minc'd-pies , [ The r ] ost-beef was laid down when she did rise ; [ Dinn ] er was ready , and lik'd so well , [ Not ] one among twenty could Joan excel ; [ They ] prais'd her so much that Joan grew proud , [ And th ] en she began to prate aloud , [ I will ] have a husband oft she cry'd , [ A prett ] y youngman to lie by my side . Then out stepped a Scot with blue bonnet on , He looked full as big as a Spanish dONE His pistol was under his chequer'd plade , His whinyard was made of a Bilbo blade : Quoth he , bred a gad , the day's mine awn , I'm as bonny a fellow as e'er was knawn , I'm will would this lassee before I'm gang beam , She'll mack me geud langkeal to fill my weam . He came salute her , but Joan was mad , And call'd the poor scotchman a saucy lad , She took up the ladle and broke his p [ a ] to , And told him he proffer'd his love too late : Deel blin you , quoth Sawny , you nasty slut , The muckl deel stop hemp in your gut , I'm now seck Joan to be my wife , She'll e'ne mack me weary of my life . Then a fine frenchman took his place , His cravet and ruffles where all of lace . Said he , begar , me comes to dis place , Me be much in love vid your sweet face , Me no like no lady vidin this town , Begar , me no like dem , they for [ y ] much frown ; Me have seen all , me tink dere's none That may be compar'd vid mistress Joan. Me be resolve to loose my life But me will have Joan to be my wife : Joan look'd about , and then reply'd , The devil shall be the french-man's bride ; March to your portage you finical knave , I'll ne'r go to France to be your slave ; Get you out of the kitchen , or else by Mars This swinging spit shall run through your a ------ Poor monsieur looked blank and sneaked away , For his wife nor his life he durst not stay ; Then enter'd and irish man and swore The noise of her beauty brought him o're ; My naam is Teague , and by my shalwaashon , I prize die faash 'bove all to the Naashon , Den preddee dear joy come kish my sweet faash , By shaint Phaatrick I never will leave this plaash . I have a potato plate of my own , And a shneeshing-boxh , 'tish very well known ; I have a schullogue to run by my shide , I fait and trote thou shalt be my bride . Be gone bogg-trotter , then Joan did cry , Or the brom-stick shall on your shoulders lie , Pack up your awl , and make short of your story , Or I'll make you pay dear for your vainglory . A seaman from Holland then enter'd the l [ is't , ] As drunken a rascal as ever pist ; He brought in his hand a bottle of Nans , And swore 'twas the famousest liquour in Fra [ nce ; ] 'Twill make you Dutch spraagen before it b [ e noon ] Be gone ( said she ) you drunken clown , I'll pull the blue rug from off your pate , If you offer to stay with Joan to prate . And then Jack Spaniard began to vapour , With a mighty short cloak and a very long rap [ ier ] He offer'd his service and proudly did strut , But Joan went and gave him a kick with her f [ oot ] You insolent dog ( quoth she ) be gone , There's none I hate more then a Spanish dONE , I mean not to wait upon such a proud sinner While he is pamp'ring his guts at dinner . The welshman hearing the rest were gone , Resolv'd that he would be with her anon , With leek in his hat , on St. Taffy's day He came to Joan , and thus he did say : Hur was a brave gentleman in Wales , Hur has a tood land , cots-plurter-a-nails , Hur has a fine goat , and hur makes sheese , Was hur makes hur a lady if now hur please . But Joan protested she hated them all , And swore to be at and english man's call ; She knew their humours and did not doubt But same or other would choose her out ; At last she married a taylor good lord , And he the greasy frigate did board , They both were well pleas'd and kindly agreed , And she from the rest of her suitors was freed .
