THE MERRY BAG-PIPES : OR , The Pleasant Pastime betwixt a Jolly Shepherd and a Country Damsel , on a Mid-Summer-Day in the Morning . To the Tune of , March Boys , etc Licensed according to Order . ( 1 ) A Shepherd sat him under a Thorn , he pulled out his Pipe and began for to play , It was on a Mid-Summers-Day in the morn , for honour of that Holiday : A Ditty he did Chant along , goes to the Tune of Cater-Bordee , And this was the Burden of his Song , if thou wilt Pipe Lad , I [ ' ] ll Dance too thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee , etc ( 2 ) And while this Harmony he did make , a Country Damsel from the Town , A Basket on her Arm she had , a gathering Rushes on the Down : Her Bongrace was of wended Straw , from the Suns hight Beams her Face is free , And thus she began when she him saw , if thou wilt Pipe Lad , I will Dance to thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee , etc ( 3 ) Then he pulled out his Pipe and began to found , while tempting on her back she lay . But when his quivering note she found , how sweetly then this Lass could play : She stopped all jumps and she revealed , she kept all time with harmony , And looking on him , sighing said , if thou wilt Pipe Lad , I will Dance to thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee , etc ( 4 ) She never so much as blushed at all , the music was so charming sweet , But ever and anon to him she'd call , and bid him be active , turn and meet : As thou art a bonny Shepherd's Swain , I am a Lass now come to woo thee , To play me another double strain , and doubt not but I will Dance to thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee , etc ( 5 ) Although I am but a silly Maid , who never was brought up at Dancing School But yet to the Jig that thou hast played , you find that I can keep time and rule : Now see that you keep your stops aright , for Shepherd I am resolved to view thee , To play me the Damsels chief delight , and never doubt but I will Dance to thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee , etc ( 6 ) The Shepherd again did tune his Pipe , and played her a Lesson loud and shrill , The Damsel his Face did often wipe , with many a thank for his good will : And said I was never so pleased before , and this is the first time that I knew thee , Come play me this very Jig once more , and never doubt but In ll Dance to thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee , etc ( 7 ) The Shepherd he said , as I am a Man , I have kept playing from Morning till noon , Thou knowest I can do no more then I can , my Pipe it is clearly out of tune : To ruin a Shepherd I'll not seek , said she , for why should I undo thee , I can come again to the Down next week , and thou shalt Pipe and I will Dance to thee , To thee , to thee , derry , derry , to thee . FINIS . Printed for C. Bates , next door to the Crown-Tavern , in West-Smithfield .
