A Cruel Cornish Murder , I briefly will declare , at your attention further , my Story wondrous rare , [ A ] and do not think this feigned , because it seems strange , What hath not Satan gained , when men from God do range ? [ ? ] tO Crowen in that County , and old blind man does dwell , Who by good peoples bounty , did live indifferent well , By name he's ca'ld Carnehewall , his house stood all alone , Where [ ke ] pt this d [ ee ] worser so cruel , the like was scarce ere known . He had a proper Damsel that lived with him , his daughter , To whom some suitors came still , and in true wedlock sought her , Because the news was bruited , how that the blind man would , Though he were poor reputed ) give forty pounds in gold . O , then bewitching money , what mischief dost thou cause , Thou makest men dote upon thee , contrary to Gods Laws . What Murder is so heinous , but thou canst find out those , Than [ tO ] willingly for gain thus , will venture life to loose . Nay often soul and body , as in this Story rare , By the sufferance of God , I will punctually declare : The fame of this men riches , a Vagrant chanced to hear , In haste his fingers itches , away the same to bear . This bloody murderous Villain , whose fact all manhood shames , Did live long time by stealing , his name was Walter James , Who with his wife , and one more young woman , and a boy , Three Innocents in purple gore , did cruelly destroy . The twenty sixth of July , when it was almost night , These wanderers unruly , on this lone house did light , The old blind man was then abroad , and none but his old wife , And a little Girl , ith' house abided , whom they deprived of life , At first they asked for Victual : quoth she , with all my heart , Although I have but little , of that you shall have part ; He swore he must have money , alas , here's none she said ; His heart then being stony , he straight cut off her head . Thus did three harmless innocents by one vile Caitiffs hand With both the counsel and consents , oth' woman of his band : Their heads and bodies laid they all very close together ; And being gone a little way , they did at last consider , That if the house were burned , the murder might be hid , With that they back returned , and as they thought , they did , Setting the house on fire , which burned till next day , Full many did admire , as they went on the way . These murderers suspected that people would have thought , Those three ith house enclosed , unto their death's were brought , By accident of fire , but God did then declare His power [ ? ] let's admire his wondrous works most rare . The murdered corpse remained , as if no fire had been , Their clothes with blood besmeared , not burned , as might be seen : The leg and arm oth' Maiden , were only burned in sunder , Full many people said then , ith' midst of their wonder . That surely there were murdered , by some that robbed them had , And presently it ordered , that for this deed so bad , All Vagrants on suspicion , should apprehended be , And in this inquisition , one happened to see , Some clothes upon the parties , that from this house we [ re ] taken And some before a Justice , the little boy told plain , All things before that passed : also the boy did say , James was ith mind to kill him , lest he should all betray , They taken were at Meriwicke , forty five miles , or more , From Crowen where the murth [ er ] er was about a month before , Where in the Jail they lay , Until the Lend Assize did come , which took their lives away [ . ] The little Boy was quit , and sent unto the Parish , Where he was born , well fitted , with clothes and food , to cherish Him , as he ought with honesty and leaves his wandering trade : The other three were doomed to die , on that which he had said . But Walter James denied , that ere he did that act , For swearing ( till he died , and when he died ) that fact His wife at her last ending , confessed the bloody guilt , So monstrously offending , when so much blood was spilled . The other woman after confessed more plainly all : James took his death with laughter and never to God did call : Thus as he lived a reprobate , and did God great reject , His soul with Christ bought at deer rate , in death he did neglect . He was hanged dead at Lancestone , among the rest that died , Then carried where the deed was done , and by the highway side , He hangs , for example , in chains now at this time , Thus have I showed the ample discourse of this foul crime . Objection may be framed , where was the old blind man : Whom I have never nam since when I first began . He was abroad ith' interim , when this mischance befell , Or else the like had happened to him , but he is living still . And goes about the Country , to beg , as he before Did use , among the Gentry , and now his need is more . All you that are kind Christians , think on this bloody deed . And crave the Lords assistance , by it to take good heed . The names of certain eminent men of the Country , for confirmation of the verity of this tragical Story . John Albon . John Coade .
