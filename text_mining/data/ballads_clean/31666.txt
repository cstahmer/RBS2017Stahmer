VAliant Jockey's march'd away , To fight the Foe , with Great Mackay ; Leaving me poor Soul , alas ! forlorn , Tocurse the hour I e'er was born : But I'm swear I'm follow too , And dearest Jockey's [ Fi ] re pursue , Near him be to Guard his precious Life , Never Scot had like a Loyal Wife : Sword I'm wear , I'm cut my Hair , Tann my Cheeks that once were thought so fair , In Soldiers Weed to him I'm speed , Never like a Trooper crossed the Tweed . Trumpet found a Victory , I'm kill my self the next Dundee ; Love and Rage , and Fate do's all agree , To do some Glorious thing by me : Great Bellona take my part , Fame and Glory steel my Heart , That for our bonny Scotland's geud , Some brave Actium may deserve my Blood : Nought shall appear of Female fear , Fighting by his side I love so dear ; All the World shall own , that ne'er was known Sike a pretty Lass this thousand Year , Now in Noble Armour bright , I'm with Courageous Heart will fight ; Fear of Death shall ne'er my Courage stain , King William's Rights I'm will Maintain : For the Glory of our Sex , I'm aw the Rebles will perplex , And let them find that Womenkind , Sometime venture with a Warlike mind : Age of Old , our Fame has told , Therefore I'm will never be controul'd ; By Friend or Foe , I'm freely go , Never was a Trooper armed so . I'm a Helmet will put on , Like a right Valiant Warlike Man , Plataeae of Steel shall guard my Back and Breast , Carbines and Pistols I'm protest , In my hand I'm cock and prime , Now and for ever is the time : While I thus am mounted Cap [ - a-pie , ] Warlike Thunder shall my Mu [ sick be , ] Let smoke arise and dim the Skies [ , ] While we do pursue the Warlik [ e ] [ prize ; ] Laurels shall Crown with true R [ enown , ] The Victory in City , Court and [ Town . ] Mars the God of War shall [ led ] The Army , that will fight and bl [ eed , ] E'er our Foe shall hope to win the [ day , ] Therefore let us march with speed [ away ; ] Hark! HAst thou such a valiant heart , To fight and take the Nations part , By the side of Jockey thy delight , For to put the Enemy to flight ? I thy Courage must commend , Yet like a true entire Friend , I would have thee stay at home , said he , For the Wars are most unfit for thee ; Moggy you are youthful and fair , Therefore can thy tender Nature hear The Shrieks and Cries which fills the Skies , As the Enemy we do surprise ? Love , said he , the loud Alarms In midst of night to Arms to A [ rms , ] Will it not affrighten thee , my [ dear , ] Should you such a sudden 'larum [ hear , ] And before the break of day , Many a valiant Soldier may , Lie in streams of reeking purple [ Gore ; ] Therefore Moggy whom I do ado [ re , ] Should'st thou be slave and I remembering [ ain , ] It would fill my Heart with muck [ le pain . ] She did reply , happy am I If I in the Bed of Honour die .
